What is Learning
Kaarvaan?

This summer, in the lap of the Himalayas students from diverse backgrounds will come together to learn in an open learning environment from their peers in the presence of an exceptional faculty of leaders.

The Learning kaarvan provides for a conducive environment over a period of x days for young adults (14-18 years) to foster and deepen their levels of self-awareness in the form of but not limited to empathetic decision-making, clearer perception, strengthened critical thinking and nurturing deeper relationships with peers and family. In order to tap into our limitless potential, we emphasize on understanding one’s self and connecting that with the world.

As teenagers transition into a life that requires of them to make informed choices in life, career or relationship paths they are faced with situations that require them to state their purpose ( think of statements of purpose), their strengths ( think of interviews and applications) and choosing fields ( think of humanities, sciences etc.). But do they have enough vantage to know themselves to make these choices authentically? Do our homes, schools or places of work nurture them to develop and build their own conscious thought? Ikigai is a japanese concept meaning a reason for being. We believe in enabling every child to discover their Ikigai.

Our Theory Of
Change


At Creatnet, we believe in the idea of life-long learning. Human beings are curious by nature and it is our eagerness to learn and grow that drives action. Our actions are reflections and manifestation of our thoughts, thus changing the outer environment starts with changing ourselves.

Hence, our programs are built upon the inherent capacity of the human beings to bring about a change in themselves, and as a result transform the very institutions and structures they are a part of.

This process holds to its core a few practices

  • Personalized learning in small groups of 10 where dialogue, discussion and questioning is of utmost importance. Where individual expression is encouraged and authenticity is valued.
  • Blossoming creativity by connecting with oneself
  • Moving from self awareness to group awareness to build empathy

Even though most people believe they are self-aware, self-awareness is a truly rare quality: Research estimates that only 10%–15% of the people we studied actually fit the criteria.

Internal self-awareness(represents how clearly we see our own values, passions, aspirations, fit with our environment, reactions and impact on others) has been associated with higher job and relationship satisfaction, personal and social control, and happiness; it is negatively related to anxiety, stress, and depression.

While research suggests that external self-awareness (understanding how other people view us) makes people more skilled at showing empathy and taking others’ perspectives.

In times of constant flux and change in terms of our work and contemporary lifestyles, we find self awareness to be a practice of essential and utmost importance. The summer program aims to flower and deepen this enquiry for each participant and hence build the next generation of conscientious young leaders

Kaarvaan Key Highlights:

Learning to Be - Self Management

  • Enabling self-awareness and observation - knowing oneself more and being aware of the environment

  • Exploring Nature finding interconnections to be; Exploring self through arts

Learning to Learn - Learning Style Identification

  • Learning to find one’s learning style, enabling creative thinking

Learning To Do - Creating Action Plans

  • Discovering our strengths, interests and exploring career paths for oneself

  • Learning to concentrate and manage time in studies and daily activities through various tools arts, yoga and meditation

Learning To Live Together - Relationship Management

  • Understanding perspectives and nurturing relationships with parents and peers

  • Effective communication and problem solving;

Activities

  • Bonfire, Rappelling, River crossing, Yoga, Spoken word poetry

  • Trek, camping
